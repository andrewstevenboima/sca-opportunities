Privacy & Data Protection Policy

Version 1.0 · Effective 21 April 2026
Aligned with: Rwanda DPPL · AU AI Framework · GDPR Principles

Plain-language summary

We collect only what we need to send you our weekly newsletter — your email, nothing else. We do not sell your data. We do not share it. You own it, you can ask us to delete it at any time, and you can unsubscribe from any email we send you.

If you just browse the site without signing up for emails, we don't collect any personal data from you at all.

This Privacy Policy is issued by Student Companion AI, the publisher of SCA Opportunities (this website). We are the data controller responsible for any personal data collected through this platform.

2. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to:

  • Anyone who visits andrewstevenboima.github.io/sca-opportunities
  • Students and others who subscribe to our weekly opportunities newsletter
  • Anyone who contacts us through the email or WhatsApp channels linked on the site

3. What data we collect

We practise strict data minimisation. We collect only what is necessary for the platform to function and, if you choose to subscribe, to send you the newsletter.

DataWhen & why
Email address Only if you voluntarily subscribe to the weekly newsletter. Stored in our Subscribers sheet along with a timestamp and the page you subscribed from.
Technical metadata Standard server logs from GitHub Pages (IP address, browser, visit time). Used only for security and availability monitoring. We do not link these to individuals.
Session cookies Minimal technical cookies required for the site to function. No advertising cookies, no tracking pixels, no third-party analytics.

What we do NOT collect: names, phone numbers, student IDs, university details, nationality, age, academic records, grades, financial data, location, or any sensitive personal data.

We do not have user accounts. There is no login, no profile, no saved history. The "bookmark" feature on opportunity cards is in-memory only and resets when you close the tab.

4. How we use your data

We use the email addresses of newsletter subscribers only to send the weekly opportunities newsletter. Full stop.

We will NEVER:

  • Sell your email to third parties
  • Share it with partner institutions, recruiters, or organizations listed on the platform
  • Use it for advertising or targeted marketing
  • Profile you based on your subscription
  • Add you to any list other than the one you subscribed to

5. Legal basis for processing

Under the Rwanda Data Protection and Privacy Law (DPPL) and GDPR principles, we process your email on the basis of your explicit consent — which you gave when you ticked the consent box on the signup form. You can withdraw that consent at any time by emailing us or unsubscribing from any newsletter email.

For standard server logs and session cookies, we rely on legitimate interests (keeping the site secure and functioning) — a narrow basis that does not involve identifying individuals.

6. Your rights

Your data belongs to you. You have the following rights, which we will honour within 30 days of your request:

Right to accessAsk us what email data we hold for you.
Right to deletionAsk us to permanently remove your email from our list.
Right to correctionUpdate your email if the one we have is wrong.
Right to portabilityGet your data in a machine-readable format.
Right to objectStop any processing you're uncomfortable with.
Right to withdraw consentUnsubscribe at any time. No questions, no friction.

To exercise any of these rights, email africa@studentcompanionai.xyz with the subject line "Data rights request". We will respond within 30 days.

7. How long we keep your data

Newsletter emailUntil you unsubscribe, then deleted within 30 days.
Server logsHeld by GitHub per their standard retention policy (typically 90 days).

8. How we protect your data

  • Storage — subscriber emails are stored in a private Google Sheet accessible only to the Student Companion AI team.
  • Transmission — all traffic between your browser and our services uses HTTPS encryption.
  • Access — restricted to authorised team members only.
  • Minimisation — we store the absolute minimum required. We have nothing to lose because we don't keep much.
  • Breach notification — in the unlikely event of a data breach affecting your email, we will notify affected subscribers within 72 hours of discovery.

9. Third parties we rely on

SCA Opportunities is a small, free platform built on infrastructure from the following providers:

ProviderWhat they do
GitHub Pages Hosts the public website. GitHub's privacy statement applies to standard server logs.
Google (Sheets & Apps Script) Stores the opportunities data and the newsletter subscriber list. Google's privacy policy applies.

We do not share your data with advertisers, recruiters, or any organisations listed as opportunities on the platform. When you click "Apply" on an opportunity, you leave our site and the privacy policy of that third-party site applies — not ours.

10. External links

Every opportunity listed on SCA Opportunities links out to a third-party website (the organisation offering the opportunity). We are not responsible for the content, accuracy, or privacy practices of those external sites. Always verify deadlines and requirements on the source site before applying.

11. Cross-border data transfers

Because we use Google and GitHub, your data may be processed in data centres outside Rwanda, including in the EU and United States. Both providers maintain GDPR-aligned safeguards and Standard Contractual Clauses that apply to such transfers.

12. Children & young people

SCA Opportunities is intended for university undergraduate students (generally aged 18+). We do not knowingly collect email addresses from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has submitted their email, please contact us immediately and we will delete it.

13. Your right to complain

If you're unhappy with how we've handled your data, we encourage you to email us first at africa@studentcompanionai.xyz so we can resolve it directly. You also have the right to lodge a formal complaint with:

  • Rwanda National Cyber Security Authority (NCSA) — www.ncsa.rw
  • EU residents — your national data protection authority

We may update this policy as the platform grows. Significant changes will be announced at the top of this page and emailed to active subscribers. The "Version" and "Effective" dates above will always reflect the current version. Continued use after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
